Rome

Explore the rise and fall of the Roman Empire.

Learn More

Overview

The Roman Empire, one of the most influential civilizations in history, dominated Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East for centuries.

History & Culture

The Roman Empire was founded in 27 BC and spanned over 500 years. It is known for its sophisticated legal system, impressive infrastructure, and impactful contributions to art, literature, and philosophy.

Timeline

  • 27 BC: The Roman Empire is founded by Augustus Caesar.
  • 476 AD: The Western Roman Empire falls.

Primary Source

“All roads lead to Rome.” - A common saying reflecting Rome's vast road network and its influence on the world.

Egypt

Discover the wonders of ancient Egypt, a land of pharaohs, pyramids, and hieroglyphics.

Learn More

Overview

Ancient Egypt was a civilization that thrived for over 3,000 years along the Nile River. Known for its impressive architecture, advanced mathematics, and rich cultural heritage.

History & Culture

Ancient Egypt was ruled by pharaohs, with a complex system of gods and goddesses, elaborate tombs, and advanced knowledge of astronomy and medicine.

Timeline

  • 3150 BC: The unification of Upper and Lower Egypt marks the beginning of the Early Dynastic Period.
  • 30 BC: The Ptolemaic Kingdom, the last independent Egyptian dynasty, is conquered by Rome.

Primary Source

“The pyramids are the only witnesses left to speak of our past.” - Egyptian Proverb.

Expert Insights

"Ancient Egypt's contributions to art, architecture, and science continue to inspire and amaze us today." - Dr. John Smith, Egyptologist

Greece

Explore the birthplace of democracy, philosophy, and the arts.

Learn More

Overview

Ancient Greece was a civilization that flourished for centuries, known for its contributions to democracy, philosophy, art, and literature.

History & Culture

Ancient Greece was divided into independent city-states, each with its own government and culture. The Greeks developed the concept of democracy, made advancements in mathematics and science, and produced iconic works of art and literature.

Timeline

  • 800 BC: The Archaic Period begins, marked by the rise of city-states and the development of the Greek alphabet.
  • 146 BC: The Roman Republic conquers the last remaining independent Greek city-states.

Primary Source

“Know thyself.” - A famous saying attributed to the Greek philosopher Socrates.

Expert Insights

"Ancient Greece's ideas and innovations have had a profound and lasting impact on Western civilization." - Dr. Mary Jones, Classicist
